Biodiesel

Make your own biodiesel with purified cooking oil

Posted by Springfield Isotopes team on March 4th, 2019

Bio, BIODIESEL

We decided to make use of the purified oil by elaborating biodiesel.

Materials

-100ml Methanol (Quickfire)
-3.5g Potassium Hydroxide
-500ml Oil
-Funnel
-Tightly sealed jar
Thermometer

Procedure

1. Measure 500 ml of cooking oil, and heat them in a pot until it reaches 130Â°F.
2. While the oil is heating up, measure 100 ml of methanol and 3.5g of potassium hydroxide.
3.Add the potassium hydroxide to the methanol and mix them gently until it disolves (WARNING: Remember to have the proper protection (eyes, nose, hands) when handling the potassium hydroxide. Before adding oil, it is TOXIC and can cause some damage to your body. If any potassium hydroxide that is not neutralized yet gets in contact with your skin, add some oil to neutralize it. Also remember to do it in an open space and add potassium hydroxide to water NOT the other way around, since it could explode). Then add the heated oil to the mixture.
4.Shake well for 5 minutes and let it rest 24 hours.
5. Perform the decantation procedure with the mix to separate the glicerine from the biodiesel.
