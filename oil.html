Wrong disposal of oil

The main reason we started this project

Posted by Sara Vargas on March 22nd, 2019

Oily consecuences

Background

According to several surveys and studies made by institutions like the AEAS, by throwing away a liter of oil you are polluting about 40,000 liters of water. This is the same amount of water the average person uses every year. Plus, oil is the main cause of the so-called "fat balls" that obstruct the collector zones. There are also economic consequences: the estimated cost of treatment for water purification, in Spain, is approximately 90 million euros per year.

So what does this project aim to achieve?

By purifying and reusing oil, we would reduce the amount of cooking oil used and disposed of. We aim for people to inform themselves about how dangerous and harmful disposing oil to the sink is by publishing a website and showing the benefits of reusing cooking oil, not only to cook again but to be more creative and make, for example, their own soap.
