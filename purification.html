Purification method

Learn how to purify your cooking oil at home!

Posted by Springfield Isotopes team on February 2019

Purify your oil!


Purifying your oil can be an eco-friendly and money saver alternative to dispose your oil. The article below will show you how to it, and you'll be able to use it 2 or 3 more times to cook or to make your own soap and/or biodiesel.

Materials

-Strainer or paper filter
-Ginger slices (20 grams per 1 liter of cooking oil)
-Jar or bottle to store the purified oil

How we did it:

1.Filter your oil with the paper filter. This will prevent from large remaining bits of food to pass through.
2. Put the oil in a heating pot and turn up the heat up to 200 °F and wait until it reaches said temperature.
3. Lower your heat and add the ginger slices one by one as the oil may jump and burn you.
4. Wait until the temperature lowers to 120 °F and then, filter again.

So, why did it work?

We separated the large remaining food bits at the beginning by filtering, and with the ginger slices we basically "cut off" the connections between the amino acids of whatever remained in the oil. Amino acids are the building blocks of proteins, and for a living organism to exist, these are needed. When we increased the temperature, we also increased the kinetic energy within the oil which augmented the number of collisions happening between the ginger slices' particles and the oil's and made the chemical reaction (purification) faster. And finally, when we filtered again, we disposed the larger bits of the ginger that remained.
