Soap!

Make your own soap with purified cooking oil

Posted by Sara Vargas on March 2nd, 2019

Soap, soap, soap!

We decided to make use of the purified oil by elaborating homemade soap

Materials

-1 L of cooking oil
-200 g Potassium Hydroxide
-400 ml of regular warm water
-Tightly sealed crystal jar
-Coconut oil and/or cinnamon for scent

Procedure

1. Measure the potassium hydroxide.

2. Add the potassium hydroxide to water, inside the jar. (WARNING: Remember to have the proper protection (eyes, nose, hands) when handling the potassium hydroxide. Before adding oil, it is TOXIC and can cause some damage to your body. If any potassium hydroxide that is not neutralized yet gets in contact with your skin, add some oil to neutralize it. Also remember to do it in an open space and add potassium hydroxide to water NOT the other way around, since it could explode)

3. Add the water and potassium hydroxide mixture to the cooking oil and start mixing, until you have a custard consistency and it is well integrated.

If you DON'T want to add scent to your soap, you may skip this step.
4. Mix in some coconut oil and/or add water to cinnamon, heat it for 2 minutes in the microwave, filter it and add it too.

5. Leave it aside in a cool place for 2 weeks before using.

Why did it work?

Soap is basically a combination of an alkali with fats or fatty acids. In this case, the potassium hydroxide is the alkali and the oil is the fat used to complete the mixture.
